The Canadian startup ecosystem has a specific set of programs worth knowing about. Each one is different in what it offers, what it expects, and what stage makes sense to apply.

Programs I've dealt with or know well:

DMZ: based at TMU (formerly Ryerson), one of the better university-linked incubators in the country. Good network, good resources if you're actually using them, application process is competitive but not insane.

IBZ: Innovation Boost Zone, more early-stage, good for when you're still figuring things out. Less prestige but more accessible.

Antler: pre-seed VC that runs cohort programs. They invest in founders, not just companies -- sometimes they form the company during the program. More intensive and more competitive.

Altitude and YSpace: good university accelerators for early-stage. Strong community but varying quality of mentorship depending on the cohort.

The hidden value of accelerators is usually the network, not the money or the curriculum. The money is small. The curriculum is often generic. But the other founders in your cohort, the investor connections, the alumni who've been through it -- that's the real asset. How much you get out depends on how much you put in.

Grants are worth pursuing for non-dilutive capital, but the process is slow. Government grants especially -- the timeline from application to cash in account can be 6-12 months. Plan accordingly. The FedDev and IRAP programs are worth looking at if you're doing anything with R&D.
